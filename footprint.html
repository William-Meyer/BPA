CARBON FOOTPRINT

WHAT IS A CARBON FOOTPRINT

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases generated by human actions. It can be measured for an individual, group, organization, or other assemblages of people.

CALCULATING A CARBON FOOTPRINT

 A carbon footprint is calculated by taking a person’s lifestyle into consideration and adding up the total emissions in terms of tonnes of carbon dioxide per year. A few of the main contributing factors to a carbon footprint are transportation choices, food consumption, energy use, and trash production.

REDUCING A CARBON FOOTPRINT

 By limiting the amount of resources (renewable and nonrenewable) an individual uses, a carbon footprint can be reduced and the long-term effects of climate change can be reduced. This will only happen, though, if every person contributes to the cause. Because of this, a carbon footprint can be a fantastic way to understand a person’s personal impact on the planet and its environment.
