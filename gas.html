Greenhouse Gases

WHAT ARE GREENHOUSE GASES?

Gases that trap heat in the atmosphere are known as greenhouse gasses. These gases influence the earth’s energy balance and cause the process called the greenhouse effect.

WHAT IS THE GREENHOUSE EFFECT

 The greenhouse effect is when energy from the sun passes through the atmosphere and heats the surface of the earth but the atmosphere prevents these gases from returning to space. This effect results in an increased average Earth temperature and is the cause of climate change. 


 There are two types of the greenhouse effect: natural and man-made. The natural greenhouse effect is what makes life on Earth possible. Heat from the sun enters the atmosphere during the day and at night, the heat returns to the atmosphere. However, greenhouse gasses keep some of this heat trapped to maintain a livable temperature on the earth’s surface.


  However, due to more and more greenhouse gases being produced by everyday human activities, the layer of gasses in the atmosphere is becoming larger and preventing enough heat from returning to space. This is called the man-made greenhouse effect and it causes warmer Earth temperatures.

SIGNIFICANTS OF GREENHOUSE GASES

 Carbon dioxide, methane, water vapor and nitrous oxide are the most abundant greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. Currently, there is 45% (1) more carbon dioxide than there was at the beginning of the industrial age. 


 The levels of greenhouse gases are increasing yearly and, without significant changes to human activities, climate change will become irreversible. However, by reducing one’s personal carbon footprint, change can begin to be made to move in the right direction.
