THE IMPACT

IMPACT OF THE INCREASED GREENHOUSE GASES

The rising amount of greenhouse gasses in the atmosphere is the largest contributor to climate change and global warming. This increase of greenhouse gases is a result of each individual’s behavior towards treating the environment, in parts of their lives such as transportation choice, food consumption, energy use and waste production.

OBSERVABLE CHANGES IN THE ENVIRONMENT

 Scientists are already seeing massive changes to the environment since the beginning of the industrial revolution, even more so nowadays. 


 Climate change has resulted in worldwide increased temperatures. As a result of these increased temperatures, glaciers have begun to shrink, resulting in increased sea levels and warmer waters, endangering the animals living in these arctic places. 


 Also, due to the higher average temperatures, farmers have had to make changes to their land to continue to produce enough food to feed the growing population. These high temperatures have also led to large amounts of property damage.


 Climate change is affecting the way all ecosystems interact and is disrupting the way of life of people worldwide.

WANT TO HELP OUT?

 There are many ways that you personally can help aid the prevention of further climate change. Every little change helps and can slow down the effects of the changing climate on the Earth.
