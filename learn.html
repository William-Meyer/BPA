What Are Greenhouse Gasses?

Greenhouse Gasses are emissions that contribute to the warming of the planet by absorbing and emitting thermal energy. The most common greenhouse gasses include methane, nitrous oxide, and carbon dioxide. When these gasses are released, they get trapped within Earth’s atmosphere. Greenhouse gasses are essential to insulating our planet, however, emissions are at an all time high which is making the global temperature rise to a dangerous level.





What Is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouses gasses emitted from a person, group, or organization. Individual carbon footprints are often made up of four categories: transportation, trash production, energy use, and food consumption. Carbon Footprint is a great way to measure your personal impact on the planet and its environment.





Why Do We Care?

Greenhouse gasses are the main contributor to global climate change. We are already starting to see the effects of climate change in an increase of severe storms and unpredictable heatwaves. In addition, arctic glaciers are melting, causing sea levels to rise. If we don’t take steps to reduce our personal and global carbon footprints we put countless ecosystems and species at risk.





How Can You Help?

Taking steps to reduce your personal carbon footprint is the best thing you can do to help fight climate change. Making simple lifestyle changes like recycling, using LED light bulbs, and driving less can significantly diminish your personal impact on the planet.
